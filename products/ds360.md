The DS360 uses digital signal processing and a precision 20-bit D/A converter to provide better than −100 dB distortion over the audio frequency range. The instrument employs direct digital synthesis architecture with meticulous shielding and board layout to minimize output noise, making it ideal for audio research, manufacturing, and automated testing.

### Frequency Stability

The DS360 delivers exceptional frequency precision of 0.0025 % across its entire range. Users can select frequencies with 6-digit resolution spanning 1 mHz to 200 kHz, with steady 25 ppm stability.

### Waveforms

The DS360 generates clean sine waves and square waves, as well as a two-tone signal for IMD testing. The instrument also outputs white noise, pink noise, and band-limited white noise. Two-tone configurations support standard formats like SMPTE, DIM, and CCIF.

### Audio Outputs

Output options include XLR, BNC, and dual banana jacks in both balanced and unbalanced configurations, with amplitudes ranging from microvolts to 80 Vpp depending on load impedance.

### Sweeps and Bursts

Linear and log sweeps are set between 10 mHz and 200 kHz with sweep times between 0.3 ms and 100 s. The instrument maintains exceptional amplitude flatness of 0.5 % to 20 kHz during frequency sweeps. Tone burst capabilities include adjustable ON cycles, repetition rates, and OFF amplitude levels.

### Computer Control

Standard IEEE-488.2 (GPIB) and RS-232 interfaces provide full computer programmability.
