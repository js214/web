The CS580 is a precision voltage-controlled current source that sources and sinks current with true four-quadrant operation. Current ranges from 100 fA to 100 mA with bandwidth up to 200 kHz and ±50 V compliance voltage.

### Performance

Adjustable gain settings from 1 nA/V to 50 mA/V provide flexibility for a wide range of applications. The instrument demonstrates exceptional output resistance of 10¹² Ω at the lowest gain setting and low thermal drift.

### Clock-Stopping Architecture

The CS580 employs a CPU Clock Stopping Architecture where the microcontroller's system clock only oscillates during the brief moments needed to change instrument settings. This eliminates digital interference when the device operates passively.

### Low-Noise Design

An actively driven guard provides the greatest bandwidth (up to 200 kHz) and lowest possible leakage current. Linear power supplies eliminate switching frequency interference. A buffered monitor output is included for high impedance voltage measurements.

### Computer Interfaces

RS-232 and optical fiber interfaces are provided. The optical fiber option connects via the SX199 controller, enabling GPIB, RS-232, and Ethernet connectivity with complete electrical isolation.
