The QCM200 reads the resonant frequency and resistance of a 5 MHz quartz crystal. As frequency changes correlate with deposited mass, the instrument can detect material accumulation in the nanogram range. The resistance measurement responds to viscosity and elasticity changes in contact films or liquids.

### Electronics Design

An automatic gain control circuit supplies necessary signal amplitude to the crystal, overcoming viscoelastic losses. The digital controller includes a frequency counter providing 0.01 Hz resolution and a resistance meter with 5-digit resolution across a 0 to 5000 Ω range. A potentiometer allows shunt capacitance cancellation for accurate series resonance operation.

### Hardware Components

The system incorporates a 5 MHz AT-cut quartz crystal wafer (1-inch diameter with circular electrodes), a compact crystal holder, three included crystals, and an optional flow cell adapter for injection analysis integration.

### EQCM Capability

For electrochemical applications, the transformer-isolated front-surface electrode interfaces directly with potentiostats or galvanostats, enabling synchronized frequency and electrochemical data collection.

### Software

Windows-based data acquisition software enables remote operation, real-time display, trend analysis, and event time-stamping for both frequency and resistance measurements.
