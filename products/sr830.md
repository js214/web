For decades, the SR830 has been the go-to choice for phase-sensitive detection in research labs. Its combination of low noise, high dynamic reserve, and simple operation has made it a fixture in experimental physics, chemistry, materials science, and engineering labs worldwide.

The SR830 uses a DSP architecture to simultaneously measure in-phase (X) and quadrature (Y) components of the signal at the reference frequency. A large 256 k-point data buffer allows long continuous measurements without a PC, and built-in GPIB and RS-232 make integration with automated test systems straightforward.
