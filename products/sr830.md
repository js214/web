For decades, the SR830 has been the first choice for phase-sensitive detection in research labs. Its combination of low noise, high dynamic reserve, and straightforward operation has made it a fixture in experimental physics, chemistry, materials science, and engineering worldwide.

### Dual-Phase DSP Detection

The SR830 uses a digital signal processing architecture to simultaneously measure the in-phase (X) and quadrature (Y) components of the input signal at the reference frequency. R and θ are computed in real time from X and Y. All four outputs are available simultaneously as analog voltages and over the digital interfaces.

### Harmonic Detection

Up to the 19th harmonic of the reference frequency can be selected for detection. Dual-harmonic mode enables simultaneous measurement at the fundamental f and any harmonic nf, useful for nonlinear characterization and pump-probe experiments.

### Data Buffer and Interfaces

A 256 k-point internal data buffer stores time, X, Y, R, and θ samples at user-defined rates. The buffer can be filled in sweep, trigger, or continuous modes and read back over GPIB or RS-232 without interrupting the measurement. Built-in IEEE-488 (GPIB) and RS-232 interfaces cover the full range of legacy and current automation environments.

### Input Flexibility

The SR830 accepts voltage input in A or A-B differential mode, or current input through an internal transimpedance amplifier. A rear-panel BNC allows the input ground to float up to 250 V DC for experiments where the signal source is not at chassis ground. A line-frequency notch filter (50/60 Hz) suppresses pickup without affecting signal bandwidth.
